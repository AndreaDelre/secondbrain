# Internal memo on tracking practices

## Current status
- Today, many of our quality tests or incidents are caused by tracking issues. We have the advantage of having an overall view of tracking, allowing us to monitor events at scale, across any dimension we choose. We need the Tracking team to handle this, using data tools rather than just front-end tools like Amplitude or the browser console.
- The apps and site services require our assistance with tagging practices and the lake ingestion process.

## Needs:
- We also need tracking support to be provided globally for everyone, not just e-commerce. And even for e-commerce, governance must be applied across all countries—monitoring all events and properties from every country to ensure consistency. We have worked on the data models for this and are supporting as many people as possible; however, we would like to speak with one voice.
- We need tracking practices to follow data best practices to guarantee a consistent model for the company.

## Proposal
- We are ready to incubate the tracking team to immerse them in data tools and accelerate this process; otherwise, we will go through another year without end-to-end testing. We don't want to manage them at all; we just want to ensure we speak with one voice and, above all, that we talk "data" and not just "front-end tools," as this has significant impacts on the company.

We do not have the bandwidth to manage them, so the concept of incubation is more about encouraging exchanges by having joint team meetings, sitting together, and sharing our tools. We believe that operating as "one team" for a period would merge practices and reduce errors while accelerating the tracking team's skill development in data tools.

I am primarily referring to tracking definition and acceptance testing (UAT).
